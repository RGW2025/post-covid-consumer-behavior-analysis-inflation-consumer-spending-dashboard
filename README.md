# post-covid-consumer-behavior-analysis-inflation-consumer-spending-dashboard
Tableau dashboard, and Google data Sheets, analyzing whether post-COVID inflation structurally changed U.S. consumer behavior.
**Title**
Post-COVID Inflation & Consumer Behavior Analysis
**Objective**
Determine whether post-COVID inflation created a permanent structural shift in U.S. consumer spending, saving, and borrowing behavior.
**Data Sources**
Real Personal Consumption Expenditures
Personal Saving Rate
Outstanding Consumer Credit
(Source: Federal Reserve Economic Data)
**Methodology**
Trimmed data starting January 2018
Combined datasets into unified time series
Indexed all variables to 100 (Jan 2018 baseline)
Built Tableau dashboard to visualize behavioral shifts
**Key Questions**
Did consumer saving return to pre-COVID levels?
Did credit usage permanently increase?
Did spending revert to historical trend?
**Tools Used**
Google Sheets (data cleaning & indexing)
Tableau (visualization)
GitHub (version control & documentation)

**Key Findings**
While COVID itself was a massive disruption that affected consumers' spending and saving activitites most predominately, everything returned to a relatively normal rate. Savings have faced the largest disruption, however, as they are lower then they were pre-COVID and have gone up and down over the past few years.
